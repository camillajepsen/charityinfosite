Trusted Charities



When selecting a charity to support, it is essential to ensure that the organization is legitimate, effective, and transparent. Your contribution should make a meaningful impact, and choosing a trustworthy charity ensures that your donations are being used responsibly.

Things to Consider When Choosing a Charity


Is the Charity Registered with the Government?


Ensure the charity is registered with the government. This confirms its legitimacy and transparency. In Australia, a registered charity also entitles you to tax benefits on donations above $2. To verify, use resources like the Australian Charities and Not-for-profits Commission (ACNC).
Check if a charity is registered in Australia (ACNC)

Does the Charity Have Transparency?


Look for charities that openly share their financials and how donations are used. Reputable organizations publish annual reports and financial statements, ensuring donors know where their money is going.

How Efficient is the Charity?

Check the charity’s overhead ratio – how much of your donation goes to programs versus administrative costs. Low overhead means more funds are used for the cause itself.
Charity Navigator – For detailed ratings of charity performance.
GiveWell – A resource for highly effective charity recommendations.

Are Reviews and Testimonials Available?

Look for independent reviews of the charity to gauge its impact and reputation. Websites like GreatNonprofits offer honest feedback from real donors.

List of Trusted Charities to Consider Supporting


Here are some well-regarded charities that make a significant impact in various areas:

For Global Health

Against Malaria Foundation – Provides bed nets to combat malaria.
Doctors Without Borders – Delivers emergency medical aid globally.

For Children and Education

Save the Children – Works to improve the lives of children through education and health initiatives.
Room to Read – Promotes literacy and gender equality in education.

For Animals

The Humane Society of the United States – Protects animals through advocacy and rescue.
World Animal Protection – Fights for animal welfare globally.

For the Environment

World Wildlife Fund (WWF) – Conserves nature and protects endangered species.
The Nature Conservancy – Focuses on global conservation efforts.

For Poverty and Humanitarian Aid

Oxfam International – Tackles poverty and inequality worldwide.
CARE International – Focuses on alleviating poverty and achieving social justice.
